The AWS Certified App offers two types of tests to help you prepare for the AWS Cloud Practitioner certification exam.

## Test Types

### Domain Mastery Tests

Domain tests focus on specific exam topics with 30 targeted questions. These tests are ideal for:
- Strengthening knowledge in specific areas
- Identifying weak points in your understanding
- Building confidence before taking the full exam

**Available domains:**
- Domain 1: Cloud Concepts (24% of exam)
- Domain 2: Security and Compliance (30% of exam)
- Domain 3: Cloud Technology and Services (34% of exam)
- Domain 4: Billing, Pricing, and Support (12% of exam)

### Full Exam Simulation

The full exam simulation replicates the real AWS certification exam experience:
- 65 randomized questions from all domains
- 90-minute time limit (realistic exam conditions)
- Official domain distribution: 16, 20, 21, and 8 questions respectively
- 15 unscored questions included (just like the real exam)

<Note>
In the real AWS exam, 15 of the 65 questions are unscored experimental questions. The app simulates this by randomly selecting which questions count toward your final score.
</Note>

## Starting a Test

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select your test type">
    From the main menu under the **Practice Tests** tab, choose either a domain test or the full exam simulation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Begin immediately">
    Click the test button to start. The timer begins immediately, so make sure you're ready before clicking.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Read each question carefully">
    Questions appear one at a time. Take your time to understand what's being asked before selecting your answer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## During the Test

### Answering Questions

Each question displays:
- The question number and total count (e.g., "Question 5 of 30")
- The question text
- Multiple choice options (labeled A, B, C, D)

<Tip>
Pay attention to whether a question asks for a single answer or multiple answers. Multi-answer questions allow up to 2 selections.
</Tip>

### Question Types

**Single-choice questions:** Select one option by clicking on it. The option will highlight in orange.

**Multiple-choice questions:** Select up to 2 options. If you try to select more than 2, you'll see a warning message.

### Eliminating Wrong Answers

To help narrow down your choices:
1. Click the "×" button next to any answer option
2. The option will be crossed out and grayed out
3. Click the "×" again to reactivate the option

<Tip>
Elimination is a powerful test-taking strategy. If you can rule out 2 wrong answers, you've increased your odds of selecting the correct one.
</Tip>

## Test Modes: Study vs. Exam

The app behaves differently depending on which test type you're taking:

### Study Mode (Domain Tests)

- **Immediate feedback:** After submitting each answer, you'll see whether you were correct or incorrect
- **Detailed explanations:** Learn why the correct answer is right
- **Sequential navigation:** Move through unanswered questions in order
- **Cannot revisit:** Once you answer a question, you cannot go back to it

<Info>
Study mode is designed for learning. Use it to understand concepts deeply rather than just memorizing answers.
</Info>

### Exam Mode (Full Simulation)

- **No immediate feedback:** Answers are not revealed until you finish
- **Full navigation:** Jump to any question at any time
- **Review before submitting:** Check all your answers before finalizing
- **Realistic conditions:** Mimics the actual AWS certification exam

<Warning>
In exam mode, you won't know if your answers are correct until you finish. This mirrors the real exam experience.
</Warning>

## Finishing Your Test

### Domain Tests

Simply work through all 30 questions. After answering the last question, you'll see your results automatically.

### Full Exam Simulation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click the Finish button">
    When ready to complete the exam, click the green **Finish** button in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review your answers">
    A review screen appears showing all questions:
    - Green border = answered
    - Red border = not answered
    
    Click any question to jump to it and add or change your answer.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm submission">
    Check the "I have reviewed all answers" checkbox, then click **Confirm Finish**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Once you confirm, you cannot change your answers. Make sure you've answered all questions before confirming.
</Warning>

## Viewing Results

After completing a test, you'll see:

**Your score:** Displayed on the AWS scale (100-1000)
- Passing score: 700 or higher
- Your score compared to the minimum

**Domain breakdown:** Performance percentage for each of the four domains
- Green bar = 70% or higher (good)
- Red bar = below 70% (needs improvement)

**Action buttons:**
- **Back to Home:** Return to the main menu
- **Retry Test:** Take the same test type again with new questions

<Tip>
AWS recommends consistently scoring 80% or higher on practice exams before attempting the real certification exam.
</Tip>

## Time Management

### Domain Tests (30 questions)

While domain tests have a 90-minute timer, you should aim to complete them much faster:
- Target: 1-2 minutes per question
- Total time: 30-60 minutes
- Use remaining time to review flagged questions

### Full Exam (65 questions)

**90 minutes total = strict time limit**

Recommended pace:
- 1.38 minutes per question on average
- First pass: 60 minutes (answer all questions quickly)
- Second pass: 20 minutes (review flagged questions)
- Final review: 10 minutes (check unanswered questions)

<Warning>
If time runs out before you finish, the exam auto-submits with whatever answers you've provided. Unanswered questions are marked incorrect.
</Warning>

## Best Practices

<Tip>
**Start with domain tests** to identify your weak areas, then focus your study efforts before attempting the full exam simulation.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Take multiple full exams** until you consistently score 700+ (preferably 800+) before scheduling your real AWS certification exam.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Track your progress** by reviewing the performance indicators on each domain test button on the main menu.
</Tip>
