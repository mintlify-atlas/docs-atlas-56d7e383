## Overview

Domain Mastery Tests let you focus your study efforts on specific areas of the AWS Cloud Practitioner exam. Each test contains 30 targeted questions from a single domain, giving you deep practice in that topic area.

<Info>
  Each domain test features **immediate feedback** after every question, so you can learn as you go.
</Info>

## How to Take a Domain Test

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Your Domain">
    From the home screen, click on any of the four domain cards under "Domain Mastery Tests". Each card shows:
    - Domain name and icon
    - Exam weight percentage
    - Number of questions (30)
    - Your last score (if you've taken it before)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Answer Questions">
    Work through the 30 questions one at a time:
    - Read the question text carefully
    - Select your answer(s) - some questions allow multiple selections
    - Click **Continue** to submit your answer
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Get Immediate Feedback">
    After each submission, you'll see:
    - Whether your answer was correct or incorrect
    - The correct answer(s) with letter labels (A, B, C, etc.)
    - A detailed explanation of why the answer is correct
    
    This immediate feedback helps reinforce learning as you practice.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete the Test">
    Once you've answered all questions, you'll see your final score displayed as a percentage. The app automatically tracks your performance for each domain.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Performance Tracking

Your most recent score for each domain appears as a circular progress indicator on the domain card:

- **Green circle** (80%+): Strong performance - you're ready for this domain
- **Red circle** (&lt;80%): Needs more study - review the concept map and retake the test

<Tip>
  AWS recommends scoring at least **80%** consistently before taking the real exam. Use the color-coded indicators to identify which domains need more attention.
</Tip>

## Study Tools During Tests

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Flag Questions">
    Mark questions for review by clicking the **flag icon** at the top of any question. Flagged questions appear highlighted in the navigation grid at the bottom.
    
    Use flags to:
    - Mark questions you want to review again
    - Highlight tricky concepts
    - Create a custom review list
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Add Notes">
    Click the **bookmark icon** to add personal notes to any question. A text area appears where you can:
    - Write reminders about key concepts
    - Note why certain answers are incorrect
    - Create study tips for yourself
    
    Questions with notes show a blue dot in the navigation grid.
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Question Navigation">
    The bottom grid shows all 30 questions:
    - **Current question** has an orange ring
    - **Correct answers** appear in green
    - **Incorrect answers** appear in red
    - **Flagged questions** have an orange background
    - **Notes indicator** shows as a blue dot
    
    <Note>
      In domain mode, you cannot navigate back to already-answered questions. The immediate feedback model keeps you moving forward.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Answer Elimination">
    For questions with multiple choice options, you can eliminate answers you know are wrong:
    
    1. Click the **×** button on any answer option
    2. The option becomes grayed out and struck through
    3. Click the × again to reactivate it
    
    This helps you narrow down choices on difficult questions.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Navigation Controls

During a domain test:

- **Previous button**: Only available for unanswered questions (you can't go back to answered ones)
- **Next button**: Moves to the next unanswered question
- **Continue button**: Submits your answer and shows immediate feedback

<Warning>
  Once you submit an answer in domain mode, you cannot change it. Take your time before clicking Continue.
</Warning>

## Best Practices

1. **Start with your weakest domain** - Use domain tests to identify gaps in your knowledge
2. **Read explanations carefully** - The immediate feedback includes valuable learning content
3. **Retake until consistent** - Keep practicing each domain until you consistently score 80%+
4. **Use the concept map** - Reference the interactive concept map (from the main menu) to understand domain relationships
5. **Take notes** - Use the notes feature to capture insights for later review

## After Completing a Test

Your score is automatically saved and displayed:
- On the domain card as a circular progress indicator
- Tracked across sessions (using browser local storage)
- Used to show your performance trend over time

Return to the home screen to select another domain or take the full exam simulation when you're ready.
