## Question Types

The exam includes two types of questions:

### Multiple-Choice Questions

These questions have **one correct answer** from four options.

**Example:**

<Info>
Which AWS service provides object storage?

A. Amazon EBS  
B. Amazon EFS  
C. Amazon S3 ✓  
D. Amazon RDS
</Info>

### Multiple-Response Questions

These questions require you to select **two or more correct answers** from five or more options. The question will always specify how many answers to select.

**Example:**

<Info>
Which are benefits of cloud computing? (Select TWO)

A. Trade capital expense for variable expense ✓  
B. Maintain physical data centers  
C. Benefit from massive economies of scale ✓  
D. Limited global reach  
E. Manual capacity planning
</Info>

<Note>
For multiple-response questions, you must select the **exact number** of correct answers specified. Partial credit is **not** awarded.
</Note>

## Exam Domains and Weighting

Questions are distributed across four domains:

| Domain | Topic | Weight | Approximate Questions |
|--------|-------|--------|----------------------|
| 1 | Cloud Concepts | 24% | ~16 questions |
| 2 | Security and Compliance | 30% | ~20 questions |
| 3 | Cloud Technology and Services | 34% | ~22 questions |
| 4 | Billing, Pricing, and Support | 12% | ~8 questions |

<Tip>
Domain 3 (Technology and Services) has the most questions, so ensure you're comfortable identifying AWS services and their use cases.
</Tip>

---

## Scored vs. Unscored Questions

Of the 65 total questions:

- **50 questions are scored** and count toward your final result
- **15 questions are unscored** (used for future exam development)

You won't know which questions are unscored, so treat every question as if it counts toward your score.

<Note>
Unscored questions are included for statistical analysis and potential use in future exam versions. They appear identical to scored questions.
</Note>

## Time Management

### Pacing Strategy

With 65 questions in 90 minutes, you have approximately **1 minute and 23 seconds per question**.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="First 30 Minutes" icon="1">
    Answer **30-35 questions**
    
    Build momentum with questions you know well
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Middle 30 Minutes" icon="2">
    Answer **20-25 questions**
    
    Work through moderate difficulty questions
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Final 30 Minutes" icon="3">
    **Complete remaining + review**
    
    Finish flagged questions and review answers
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Time Management Tips

1. **Don't get stuck** on difficult questions - flag them and move on
2. **Read carefully** but efficiently - eliminate obviously wrong answers first
3. **Leave 10-15 minutes** for review at the end
4. **Answer every question** - there's no penalty for wrong answers
5. **Use the flag feature** to mark questions for review

<Tip>
If you're unsure about a question, make your best guess and flag it for review. Never leave a question unanswered.
</Tip>

## Test Delivery Options

### Testing Center (Pearson VUE)

- In-person proctored exam at a physical testing center
- Requires valid ID and adherence to strict testing center policies
- Scheduled appointments available worldwide
- Results available immediately after completion

### Online Proctored (Pearson VUE OnVUE)

- Take the exam from home or office
- Requires webcam, microphone, and stable internet connection
- Room must be private and free of distractions
- System check required before exam
- Live proctor monitors you via webcam throughout

<Info>
**Online Testing Requirements:**
- Windows or macOS computer (no tablets or mobile devices)
- Webcam and microphone (built-in or external)
- Stable internet connection (minimum 1 Mbps)
- Private, quiet room with closed door
- Clean desk with no study materials visible
</Info>

## During the Exam

### Interface Features

- **Question counter**: Shows current question number and total
- **Timer**: Displays remaining time
- **Flag button**: Mark questions for later review
- **Review screen**: See all questions and their status before submitting
- **Navigation**: Move forward, backward, or jump to specific questions

### Allowed During Exam

✅ Calculator (provided in exam interface)  
✅ Flag and review questions  
✅ Change answers before submitting  
✅ Take bathroom breaks (timer continues)  
✅ Request assistance from proctor

### NOT Allowed During Exam

❌ Study materials, books, or notes  
❌ Electronic devices (phones, smartwatches)  
❌ Food or drinks (testing center)  
❌ Talking or noise-making  
❌ Looking away from screen for extended periods  
❌ Unauthorized people in room (online testing)

<Note>
For online proctored exams, the proctor can see your screen and you via webcam. They may pause your exam if they observe any policy violations.
</Note>

## After the Exam

### Immediate Results

- **Pass/Fail status**: Displayed on screen immediately
- **Preliminary report**: Available in your AWS Certification account within 5 business days
- **Detailed score report**: Shows performance by domain
- **Digital badge**: Issued within 5 business days after passing

### Score Interpretation

Scores range from 100-1000, with a minimum passing score of **700**.

| Score Range | Interpretation |
|-------------|----------------|
| 700-799 | Passing - Solid understanding |
| 800-899 | Strong performance |
| 900-1000 | Excellent mastery |

<Info>
AWS uses **scaled scoring** to account for variations in exam difficulty. A score of 700 represents the same level of competence across different exam versions.
</Info>

### If You Don't Pass

- **14-day waiting period** before you can retake the exam
- Review your score report to identify weak domains
- Focus additional study on areas where you scored lowest
- Use this app's **Domain Mastery Tests** to practice specific topics
- No limit on number of retake attempts (but each attempt costs $100)

## Certification Validity

The AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner certification is valid for **3 years** from the date you pass the exam.

### Recertification Options

1. **Retake the Cloud Practitioner exam** before expiration
2. **Pass a higher-level AWS certification** (Associate or Professional level)
3. **Earn 40 continuing education credits** through AWS training and events

<Tip>
Passing any Associate-level AWS certification (Solutions Architect, Developer, or SysOps Administrator) automatically recertifies your Cloud Practitioner credential.
</Tip>
